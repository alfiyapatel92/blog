What is Menopause?

Menopause is a natural biological process that marks the end of a woman's reproductive years. It occurs when the ovaries stop producing estrogen and progesterone, leading to the cessation of menstruation.

Menopause is typically diagnosed when a woman has gone without a period for 12 consecutive months. It is a normal part of aging and usually occurs between the ages of 45 and 55, although it can happen earlier or later.

During menopause, women may experience a range of symptoms, including hot flashes, mood changes, sleep disturbances, vaginal dryness, and more. However, not all women experience symptoms, and there are various treatments available to manage them.

If you're experiencing menopause symptoms, it's important to talk to your healthcare provider. They can help you determine the best course of treatment based on your individual needs.
